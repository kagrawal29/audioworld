## 2. TOUCHPOINT DEFINITION — ORIGINAL VS CURRENT

### Original Definition (Mar 1–14)
All actions counted: connection requests (CRs), DMs, comments, **profile visits, likes, reactions, follows.**

### Revised Definition (confirmed Mar 15, 2026)
Only **CRs + DMs + comments** count as touchpoints. Profile visits, likes, reactions, and follows are "supporting actions" — they build visibility but do NOT count toward targets.

### Impact on Numbers

| Metric | Old Definition (estimate) | New Definition (exact) |
|--------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| Per sprint (avg) | ~80–120 | ~24 (early) → ~38 (late March) |
| March total | ~900–1,100 est. | **311** |
| Month target | 500 (original) | 402 (revised) |

**Why the change mattered:** The old definition inflated optics but didn't reflect actual outreach quality. A profile visit doesn't open a conversation. The new definition tracks only actions that can generate a reply.

## 3. STRATEGY EVOLUTION — THREE PHASES

### Phase 1: Manual Segment Rotation (Mar 4–20)
**Approach:** Daily sprints targeting a rotating segment (Ad Agencies → Corporate Video → Animation → repeat). Segments chosen from a pre-built spreadsheet plan. Operator executed manually with lead-approval gates.

**What we learned:**
- Segment rotation produced inconsistent quality. An "Ad Agency" sprint could reach a VO artist or a Creative Director — no profile-level filter.
- Low CR volume (0–13/day) because we hadn't activated Sales Navigator yet.
- DM misrouting incidents (compose overlay bug) — first identified Mar 18.
- Volume was capped by manual prep time.

**Notable wins:** Sagar Sahu (S5 call Mar 21), Girish Singh (warm S4), Joby Jose (existing client re-engaged), Riya M. direct requirement discovered.

### Phase 2: Sales Navigator Activated + Auto-Sprints (Mar 16–20)
**Changes:** Sales Nav Core purchased and onboarded Mar 18. Unlimited personalized CRs (300-char notes) via Sales Nav overflow menu. Auto-sprints authorized Mar 15 — daily execution without manual approval gate.

**What we learned:**
- Sales Nav CRs dramatically improved quality AND volume. Mar 19 first CR blitz: 13 CRs, 31 total touchpoints.
- CR acceptance rate jumped. 14 incoming CRs accepted Mar 19 alone.
- But segment-based targeting still meant mixed profile quality.
- Mar 20: 5 of 13 CRs sent to poor-fit profiles (post-production people, not decision-makers). Antara flagged this.

### Phase 3: Tier 1 ICP Framework (Mar 21–present)
**Trigger:** Strategy revamp meeting with Ayush + Antara feedback on Mar 20 sprint quality.

**Change:** Dropped segment rotation entirely. Replaced with **profile-based targeting** using a Tier 1 ICP definition:

> **ICP:** Founders / Executive Producers / Creative Directors / Heads of Production at small-to-mid production houses and creative agencies, pan-India. They make ad films, TVCs, branded content, documentaries, explainers — content needing VO/dubbing/audio. They **buy** these services.

**Sub-tiers by intent signal:**
- **1A (Active Intent):** Posted a direct requirement (VO/dubbing hiring, partner search). ~100% response rate. Always Priority 1.
- **1B (Production Signal):** Posted about a recent project or campaign. ~20–25% response rate. 40–45% of volume.
- **1C (Profile Fit, No Signal):** Right title, right company, no recent post. ~8–10% response rate. 55–60% of volume.

**Results after 5 Tier 1 sprints (Mar 23–30):**
- Average: 33 tp/sprint vs 22 in Phase 2
- Multiple S4 leads from 1A signals (Mikhil Musale, Ahmed Khan, Bulbul Biswas)
- First sub-tier conversion data: 1A > 1B > 1C as expected
